Summary

OL (Owl Lisp) is a purely (mostly) functional dialect of Lisp. It implements an extended subset of R5RS Scheme but has been renamed as a Lisp by its author mainly to avoid possible confusion.

You can immediately try Ol in the provided terminal on this page. For example, type: 

(+ 3/7 2+4i)
Or you can try more interesting example 
(fold * 1 (iota 1 1 100))
which is another way of 
(let factorial ((n 99))
   (if (= n 1)
      1
      (* n (factorial (- n 1)))))
This must output 99! for you.
